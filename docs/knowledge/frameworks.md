# International Labor Rights Frameworks

Reference document for the HRDD Helper AI system. These frameworks are used to analyze and document labor rights violations.

## ILO Conventions (International Labour Organization)

### Core Conventions (Fundamental Principles)
- **C087** — Freedom of Association and Protection of the Right to Organise (1948)
- **C098** — Right to Organise and Collective Bargaining (1949)
- **C029** — Forced Labour (1930) + Protocol of 2014
- **C105** — Abolition of Forced Labour (1957)
- **C138** — Minimum Age (1973)
- **C182** — Worst Forms of Child Labour (1999)
- **C100** — Equal Remuneration (1951)
- **C111** — Discrimination (Employment and Occupation) (1958)
- **C155** — Occupational Safety and Health (1981)
- **C187** — Promotional Framework for Occupational Safety and Health (2006)

### Governance Conventions
- **C081** — Labour Inspection (1947)
- **C122** — Employment Policy (1964)
- **C129** — Labour Inspection (Agriculture) (1969)
- **C144** — Tripartite Consultation (1976)

### Relevant Sectoral Standards
- **C184** — Safety and Health in Agriculture (2001)
- **C170** — Chemicals Convention (1990)
- **C176** — Safety and Health in Mines (1995)

## OECD Guidelines for Multinational Enterprises

### Key Chapters
- **Chapter II** — General Policies (disclosure, human rights, environment)
- **Chapter IV** — Human Rights (due diligence, adverse impacts)
- **Chapter V** — Employment and Industrial Relations (freedom of association, collective bargaining, non-discrimination, forced labour, child labour)

### OECD Due Diligence Guidance
Six-step framework:
1. Embed responsible business conduct into policies
2. Identify and assess adverse impacts
3. Cease, prevent, or mitigate adverse impacts
4. Track implementation and results
5. Communicate how impacts are addressed
6. Provide for or cooperate in remediation

## UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights (UNGPs)

### Three Pillars
1. **State Duty to Protect** human rights (Principles 1-10)
2. **Corporate Responsibility to Respect** human rights (Principles 11-24)
3. **Access to Remedy** for victims (Principles 25-31)

### Key Principles for HRDD
- **Principle 15** — Human rights policy commitment
- **Principle 17** — Human rights due diligence process
- **Principle 18** — Assessing human rights impacts
- **Principle 19** — Integrating findings and taking action
- **Principle 20** — Tracking effectiveness
- **Principle 22** — Remediation of adverse impacts
- **Principle 29** — Operational-level grievance mechanisms

## EU Corporate Sustainability Due Diligence Directive (CSDDD)

### Scope
- Large EU companies (>1000 employees, >€450M turnover)
- Non-EU companies with significant EU turnover
- Covers own operations, subsidiaries, and business partners (value chain)

### Requirements
- Identify, prevent, mitigate, and account for adverse human rights and environmental impacts
- Adopt a due diligence policy
- Establish complaints procedure
- Monitor effectiveness
- Public communication
- Civil liability for failure to prevent adverse impacts

### Implementation Timeline
- 2027: Companies >5000 employees, >€1.5B turnover
- 2028: Companies >3000 employees, >€900M turnover
- 2029: Companies >1000 employees, >€450M turnover

## FSC Standards (Forest Stewardship Council)

### Relevant Principles
- **Principle 2** — Workers' Rights and Employment Conditions
  - 2.1: Uphold ILO Declaration on Fundamental Principles
  - 2.2: Promote gender equality
  - 2.3: Health and safety
  - 2.4: Fair wages and conditions
  - 2.5: Freedom of association
  - 2.6: Grievance mechanisms
- **Principle 3** — Indigenous Peoples' Rights
- **Principle 4** — Community Relations

### Controlled Wood Standard (FSC-STD-40-005)
- Risk assessment for labor rights violations
- Unacceptable activities: forced labor, child labor, discrimination, violation of freedom of association

## Relevance to HRDD Helper

The AI assistant uses these frameworks to:
1. **Identify** which conventions/standards may apply to a reported situation
2. **Classify** the type of violation (freedom of association, forced labor, discrimination, health & safety, etc.)
3. **Guide** documentation with framework-specific questions
4. **Generate** reports that reference applicable standards
5. **Recommend** next steps based on framework procedures (complaints, grievance mechanisms, remediation)

The system does NOT provide legal advice. It provides structured documentation and framework references that union organizers and legal teams can use.
