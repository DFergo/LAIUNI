# Session Summary — Officer

Generate a summary for the officer based on the full conversation. This summary is shown to the user in the chat. Write it in the user's language.

---

# Instructions

**Language:** Professional, institutional-grade, precise. Officers expect substantive analysis they can use in negotiations, board reports, and strategic decisions.

**Structure the summary as follows:**

1. **Case overview:** Company, location, sector, scope (workers, sites, countries), timeline, violation type. If this involves a multinational, note the corporate structure.

2. **Framework analysis:**
   - ILO conventions engaged — with specific articles where relevant
   - OECD provisions — chapters, due diligence steps applicable
   - CSDDD — scope assessment, enforcement timeline, responsible authority
   - FSC — certification status, audit cycle, complaint mechanism
   - UNGP — pillars and principles engaged
   - Company's own commitments — code of conduct, sustainability reports, public statements

3. **Severity and strategic assessment:** Evaluation of the case strength, including:
   - Severity of the violation
   - Quality and completeness of evidence
   - Strategic significance (precedent value, sector impact, campaign potential)
   - Political and legal context

4. **Supply chain dimension:** If applicable — parent company, brand customers, investors, value chain position, CSDDD exposure.

5. **Recommended strategy:**
   - Which mechanisms to pursue and in what order
   - What additional evidence or documentation is needed
   - Coordination requirements (national union, UNI, other GUFs)
   - Timeline considerations
   - Risk factors

6. **Open questions:** Issues that require further analysis, information, or decision.

**Tone:** Collegial, strategic, precise.

**Length:** As detailed as the case requires. Institutional submissions need substance.

**Do not include:** Session integrity flags or raw internal assessment notes. Those belong in the internal UNI document.
